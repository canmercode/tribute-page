Ghenkis Khan

The first Great Khan, Emperor of the Mongol Empire, and the father of Asia

Here's a timeline of Ghenkis Khan's life:

  • c. 1162 - Born Temüjin Borjigin in the Khentii mountains.
  • c. 1171 - A young Temüjin's father is assassinated by a poisoning at the hands of the Tatars, leaving him and his family destitute.
  • c. 1184 - Rescues his abducted wife Börte from the Merkit with the help of his blood brother Jamukha and Wang Khan.
  • c. 1185 First son Jochi is born; leading to doubt about his paternity later among Genghis's children, being born shortly after Börte's rescue from the Merkits.
  • 1190 Unites the Mongol tribes, becomes leader, and devises a code of law known as Yassa.
  • 1201 Finalizes the union of the tribes by defeating Jamukha and his Jaradans.
  • 1202 Adopted as Wang Khan's heir after successful campaigns against the Tatars.
  • 1203 Victory over Wang Khan's Keraites. Wang Khan himself killed by accident by allied Naimans.
  • 1204 Victory over Naimans (all these confederations unite and become the Mongols).
  • 1206 Former childhood friend and blood brother Jamukha rejects Temüjin's proposed renewal of their brotherhood and requests a noble's death without bloodshed. He is buried with the golden belt given to him by Temüjin when they first formed their bond of brotherhood.
  • 1207-1210 Genghis leads operations against the Western Xia, which comprises much of northwestern China and parts of Tibet. The Western Xia ruler submits to him. During this period, the Uyghurs also submitted peacefully to the Mongols and became valued administrators throughout the empire.
  • 1211 After the kurultai, Genghis leads his armies against the Jin dynasty ruling northern China.
  • 1215 After conquering Bejing, Genghis Khan turns to the west and the Khara-Kitan Khanate.
  • 1219-1222 Conqueres the Khwarezmid Empire, a Persinate Sunni Muslim dynasty located in central Asia and modern Iran.
  • 1226 Starts a campaign against the Western Xia for forming a coalition against the Mongols, the second battle with the Western Xia.
  • 1227 Dies after conquering the Tangut people. Cause of death uncertain.
